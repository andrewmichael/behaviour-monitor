    Then implement two methods on CorrelationDetector:

    1. **`decay_stale_pairs(self) -> None`** — Remove pairs from `_pairs` that are NOT in `_learned_pairs` AND have `co_occurrences < self._min_observations`. This prunes noise pairs that never accumulated enough evidence to be useful. Called at the end of `recompute()` so learned status is determined first.

    Per D-01 (Claude's discretion): Use the PMI-threshold approach — pairs are pruned when they fail to meet the promotion criteria during daily recompute. This is natural (no new timers, no explicit time tracking) and leverages the existing daily batch cycle. Pairs that have enough observations but low PMI are kept because their statistics are still meaningful for future recomputation if behavior changes.

    Per D-03 (Claude's discretion on aggressiveness): PMI-threshold based pruning with no explicit time limit. Pairs that accumulate enough co-occurrences are retained regardless of recency. Only noise pairs (below min_observations and not learned) are removed. This is conservative — it avoids prematurely removing pairs that might become correlated again.

    2. **`remove_entity(self, entity_id: str) -> None`** — Per D-02: Remove all state associated with a specific entity:
       - Delete all pairs from `_pairs` where `entity_id` is in the pair's `entities` tuple
       - Delete matching entries from `_learned_pairs`
       - Remove entity from `_entity_event_counts`, subtract its count from `_total_event_count`
       - Remove entity from `_break_cycles`

    3. **Modify `recompute()`** — Add `self.decay_stale_pairs()` call at the very end, after `self._learned_pairs = new_learned`.

    Do NOT add any Home Assistant imports. These are pure Python methods.
